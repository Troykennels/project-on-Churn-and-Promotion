PROMOTION PROJECT INSIGHTS (Data Understanding Employee Performance Promotion Trends Model Findings)

  1. Promotion Rate Is Very Low Promotion distribution chart shows: 0 = Not Promoted VERY high count 1 = Promoted Very low count This indicates a highly imbalanced dataset, meaning: Only a small percentage of employees are promoted. Promotions are selective and competitive. HR likely uses strict criteria. Implication: Models must handle imbalance (e.g., class weights or SMOTE), from an HR standpoint, this suggests a narrow promotion funnel.

  2. Division Strongly Influences Promotion Division vs Promotion chart shows: Some divisions (e.g., Commercial, Customer Service) have many employees but few promotions. Smaller specialized divisions (like Research, IT Solutions, Strategy) show higher promotion ratios compared to their size. Insight: Promotion chances differ across departments, employees in strategic, technical, or specialized units appear more likely to be promoted. Business Impact: Possible skill gaps or unequal opportunity distribution.

  3. Qualification Plays a Major Role From the dataset: Higher degrees (MSc, MBA, PhD) correlate with higher promotion likelihood. Lower qualifications (HND/First Degree) show fewer promotions. Missing qualifications were rare but filled Insight: Organization seems to value advanced education in promotion decisions. Recommendation: Upskilling programs may improve promotion mobility.

  4. Performance Scores Are Critical There are two important performance metrics:

  5. Last Performance Score

  6. Training Score Average Employees with high training scores and high-performance review scores are significantly more likely to be promoted. Insight: Promotion is performance-driven, not tenure-based. Recommendation: HR should monitor performance development programsthese directly affect promotion readiness.

  7. Trainings Attended + Targets Met Matter Employees who attend more trainings and meet targets consistently tend to have better promotion odds. Insight: The organization promotes employees who demonstrate continuous improvement and goal achievement.

  8. Year of Recruitment (Tenure Effect) Employees hired in earlier years (older tenure) show slightly higher promotion frequency. However, this is not linear meaning promotion is not simply based on time spent, but is influenced along with performance and qualification. Insight: Experience is helpful but not enough alone.

  9. Foreign Schooled Employees Show Higher Promotion Odds Based on the variables: Foreign_schooled = Yes Higher likelihood as this may indicate perceived superior training or wider experience. Insight: The organization values global exposure / external education.

  10. Disciplinary Actions Reduce Promotion Chances Employees with Past_Disciplinary_Action = Yes have almost zero promotions. Insight: Behavioral history is a strong negative factor.

  11. Marital Status Has Minimal Impact Marital status (Married/Single) does not significantly affect promotion. Insight: This is expected HR typically avoids bias regarding marital status. OVERALL SUMMARY INSIGHT Promotion in this company is largely determined by performance indicators, qualifications, training participation, and division type, with minimal influence from demographic variables. Employees who score highly in:

  12. Performance reviews

  13. Training outcomes

  14. Target achievements are most likely to be promoted. Employees with:

  15. lower education

  16. disciplinary history

  17. low training participation are least likely.

STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

  1. HR should strengthen performance-improvement programs
  2. Training quality and frequency show strong promotion relevance.
  3. Identify divisions with poor promotion ratios They may require: Skill upgrades Structural review Managerial training
  4. Consider fairness policies and large gaps across divisions may indicate unequal opportunity distribution.
  5. Create transparent promotion criteria, employees should know what variables truly drive promotion.
  6. Boost promotion pipeline for high-performing employees in low-promotion units and also unlock hidden talent.

import pandas as pd
from sklearn.model_selection import train_test_split
from sklearn.preprocessing import LabelEncoder
from sklearn.ensemble import RandomForestClassifier
import joblib

# Load cleaned dataset
df = pd.read_csv("promotion_dataset.csv")

# Encode categorical columns
le = LabelEncoder()
for col in df.columns:
    if df[col].dtype == "object":
        df[col] = le.fit_transform(df[col])

# Features and target
X = df.drop("Promoted_or_Not", axis=1)
y = df["Promoted_or_Not"]

# Train-test split
X_train, X_test, y_train, y_test = train_test_split(
    X, y, test_size=0.3, random_state=42, stratify=y
)

# Train Random Forest model
model = RandomForestClassifier(
    n_estimators=300, random_state=42
)
model.fit(X_train, y_train)

# Save model
joblib.dump(model, "promotion_model.pkl")
joblib.dump(le, "label_encoder.pkl")  # Save encoder (if needed)
